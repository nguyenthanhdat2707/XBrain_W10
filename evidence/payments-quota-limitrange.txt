# Payments ResourceQuota and LimitRange evidence

Commands:

kubectl get resourcequota payments-quota -n payments
kubectl describe resourcequota payments-quota -n payments
kubectl get limitrange payments-container-defaults -n payments
kubectl describe limitrange payments-container-defaults -n payments
kubectl apply --dry-run=server -f tests/payments/invalid-quota-pod.yaml
kubectl apply --dry-run=server -o yaml -f tests/payments/invalid-no-limits.yaml | sed -n '/resources:/,/terminationMessagePath:/p'

Expected output:

- ResourceQuota exists and shows hard limits for pods, requests.cpu, requests.memory, limits.cpu, and limits.memory.
- LimitRange exists and shows default requests/limits for containers.
- invalid-quota-pod is rejected by ResourceQuota because the pod exceeds payments quota.
- invalid-no-limits is defaulted by LimitRange with cpu/memory requests and limits before validating admission.
